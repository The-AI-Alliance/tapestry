# Governance & Participation Work Group

## Purpose

Translate Tapestry's governance principles into operating mechanics for work groups, participants, contributions, decisions, certification processes, and anti-capture safeguards.

## Why it exists

Governance is load-bearing for Tapestry's architecture. [Design Goal 3](../../architecture/4-design-goals.md) requires anti-capture, [Design Goal 5](../../architecture/4-design-goals.md) requires participation to be strategically rational, and [`../../governance/`](../../governance/README.md) holds the governance design docs this group must operationalize.

## Scope

- Contribution credit and decision-rights models across data, compute, engineering, evaluation, and governance work.
- Work-group operating process: charters, ownership, escalation, decision records, and review cadence.
- Anti-capture safeguards for funding, compute, data, model access, certification, and roadmap influence.
- Certification process governance: who evaluates, who certifies, how disputes are handled.
- Interfaces between technical work groups and formal governance documents.

Out of scope: implementing technical controls, selecting benchmarks, or defining data licenses in detail.

## Initial questions

- How does Tapestry reward major contributors without letting contribution size become unilateral control?
- What decisions belong in work groups, ADRs, governance docs, or consortium votes?
- How are conflicts resolved between participant sovereignty and shared-base safety requirements?
- What certification process is credible without making the AI Alliance a kingmaker?

## Early deliverables

- A work-group operating model and decision-escalation path.
- Contribution categories and credit/accounting requirements.
- Anti-capture review checklist for architecture and operations decisions.
- Certification governance proposal coordinated with Evaluation & Certification.

## Interfaces

- **Data Governance:** data contribution rights and benefit sharing.
- **Base Model Training:** contribution weighting and shared-model decision rights.
- **Evaluation & Certification:** certification authority, appeals, and public claims.
- **Infrastructure & Operations:** contribution accounting and transparent operations.
- **Deployment & Adoption:** participant rollout priorities and public positioning.
