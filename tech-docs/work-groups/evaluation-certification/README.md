# Evaluation & Certification Work Group

## Purpose

Define the evidence that Tapestry models, pipelines, and participants must produce before claims of capability, sovereignty, cultural alignment, safety, or certification are accepted.

## Why it exists

Tapestry's value proposition depends on credible proof, not assertion: frontier capability without sacrificing sovereignty, cultural alignment that belongs to the community, and certification that can support strategic positioning. This work group supports [Design Goal 5](../../architecture/4-design-goals.md), [Design Goal 8](../../architecture/4-design-goals.md), and the certification value proposition in [Phase 3](../../architecture/3-value-propositions.md).

## Scope

- Capability benchmarks and regression suites.
- Cultural alignment and domain-specific evaluation design.
- Safety baseline checks and release gates.
- Certification criteria for sovereign AI claims.
- Audit evidence requirements and reporting formats.

Out of scope: implementing training or serving stacks, deciding governance representation, or owning raw data provenance.

## Initial questions

- What must a model demonstrate before it is called Tapestry-derived or Tapestry-certified?
- How should cultural alignment be measured without flattening culture into simplistic benchmark scores?
- Which evaluations are global baseline gates, and which are participant-specific?
- What evidence should be public, private to the consortium, or retained only by the participant?

## Early deliverables

- A minimum evaluation matrix for MVP models.
- Draft certification criteria for data sovereignty, alignment quality, safety, and portability.
- A release-gate checklist for shared-base and sovereign-alignment updates.
- Audit evidence requirements for Data Governance, Security & Privacy, and Governance & Participation.

## Interfaces

- **Sovereign Alignment:** cultural and instruction-quality evaluations.
- **Base Model Training:** shared-base capability and regression testing.
- **Data Governance:** provenance evidence and dataset restrictions.
- **Security & Privacy:** privacy and safety evidence.
- **Governance & Participation:** certification process and dispute handling.
- **Deployment & Adoption:** user feedback as evaluation signal.
